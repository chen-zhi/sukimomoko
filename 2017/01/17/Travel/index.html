Let’s get started

You’re about to start a truly magical adventure. Learning Japanese, (or ‘nihongo’ in Japanese, written ‘日本語’) will help you realize your dreams, and open doors you never knew existed.

It’s a huge challenge. Mastering the Japanese language will take many hours of hard work. You’ll need to learn three brand new writing systems, a new way of thinking about sentence structure, and a new way of pronouncing words. Not to mention you’ll need to be familiar with a whole new culture in order to be able to communicate properly with native speakers 😨

But never fear! Here at LinguaLift, we are with you every step of the way. We’ll break down what is a huge, daunting (but exciting!) task into manageable chunks 😎

What’s more, because self-teaching a language can at times be quite a challenge for even the brightest and most talented of learners, the LinguaLift expert study coaches and helpful language tutors will be checking in from time to time to make sure that you’re on the right track.

We are here whenever you need us. You can send us a message whenever you feel like you need a little guidance.

Find your motivation

While one can learn Japanese just for the sake of it, it rarely leads to good results. You need to set a specific goal for why you want to learn the language. ☝️

It can be anything—a dream of working in Japan, understanding Japanese songs and restaurant menus, or even meeting a Japanese girlfriend or boyfriend—what’s important is that you believe and stick to it on the way to master the language.

What’s important is that you believe in your dream, and stick to it.
By doing this, you’ll always be reminded of why you started learning 日本語 (nihongo). Whenever you feel like quitting, or taking a day off from studying, you can remind yourself of your original motivation to learn this exciting language, and rediscover the motivation you need to succeed 😊

Language is culture

An exciting part of learning Japanese are set expressions, that often have no equivalent in English.

Japan has centuries-old traditions, culture-specific speech patterns, and untranslatable phrases that are widely used every day. Learning these words and phrases will unlock a window into the Japanese way of life.

One part of Japanese culture that you will find in every household are the greetings used when people leave the house or come home.

You’ll hear four set expressions, depending on whether you are the person leaving or coming home, or the person greeting the person leaving or coming home. We’ll learn these four phrases in this lesson.
